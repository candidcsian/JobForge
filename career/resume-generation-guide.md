## Resume Length Guidelines

### 1-Page Resume (Not Recommended for 16+ years)
- Use only for very specific roles
- Focus on last 5 years
- Highlight top 3-5 achievements

### 2-Page Resume (Recommended)
- **Page 1**: Summary, Skills, Recent Experience (2020-2026)
- **Page 2**: Earlier Experience (2010-2020), Education, Certifications

### 3-Page Resume (For Senior/Lead Roles)
- **Page 1**: Summary, Skills, Key Achievements
- **Page 2**: Recent Experience (2019-2026)
- **Page 3**: Earlier Experience (2010-2019), Education, Certifications, Publications

## ATS Optimization Tips

1. **Use standard section headers**: Professional Summary, Skills, Experience, Education
2. **Include keywords** from job description naturally
3. **Avoid tables, graphics, columns** (use simple formatting)
4. **Use standard fonts**: Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman
5. **Include both acronyms and full forms**: "CI/CD (Continuous Integration/Continuous Deployment)"
6. **Quantify achievements** with numbers
7. **Use action verbs**: Led, Built, Implemented, Achieved, Reduced, Increased
8. **Include years of experience** for each skill
9. **List certifications** with year obtained
10. **Use .docx or .pdf** format (check job posting preference)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Too much detail** on old projects (pre-2015)
2. **Missing quantified achievements** (always include numbers)
3. **Generic statements** ("Responsible for testing")
4. **Typos and grammatical errors**
5. **Inconsistent formatting**
6. **Missing keywords** from job description
7. **Too technical** (balance technical depth with readability)
8. **No context** for achievements (always explain impact)
9. **Outdated skills** prominently featured (QTP/UFT should be de-emphasized)
10. **Missing contact information** or LinkedIn profile
